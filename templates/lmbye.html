Upload an image and let our model describe what it sees.

What is this?

Let Me Be Your Eyes uses a YOLO computer vision model to detect and identify objects in images. It is designed as an accessibility tool that helps users understand what is in a photo, a scene, or their surroundings. In this demo, a user uploads an image, and the model analyzes it to identify objects and outline them directly in the picture. In a future version, the system could run live on a phone’s camera, allowing a user to point their camera at the world around them and speak the name of an object they want to reach. The model would analyze the live video feed and verbally guide the user around obstacles, helping them navigate safely to their destination.

How it works

  • 01

    Upload an image

    A photo of a room, hallway, or any scene (Accepts JPG, PNG, and WEBP formats).

  • 02

    Passes through a neural network

    YOLO processes the entire image in a single forward pass, dividing it into a grid and simultaneously predicts what objects are present, their location and how confident it is in each detection.

  • 03

    Results are returned to you

    Every detected object is labeled and outlined giving a readable picture of the space.
